Steve Jobs



Founding Apple

Steve Jobs is one of the most important people in the history of computers. He was born on February 25, 1955 and died on October 5, 2011. He founded Apple Computer with his friends Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne out of Job's garage. His first computer, the Apple I, was made in 1976. In 1977, he managed to obtain the financing, distribution, and publicity for the company Apple Computer, and thus Apple was created. The same year he launched Apple Computer, he released the Apple II. The Apple II became a huge success, making Apple $139 million dollars.

Contributions

Steve Jobs helped found Apple, but he also changed electronics forever. In addition to the Apple computers, he created the iPod, iPad, and iPhone. In 1988, he released the iMac, now known as the MacBook. The computer sold about 5 million units within 3 years of it's launch. He changed what people thought computers were, making them smaller than before. He also made them more affordable and cheaper to buy, with the iMac costing $1,299, while alternatives cost over $1,700. He also bought Pixar in 1986, which is now known as an innovative and critically aclaimed animation studio. Without Steve Jobs, electronics may not be like how they are now, with him and Apple changing the industry forever.
